RiskCueBench

A Benchmark for Risk Cue Recognition in Safety-Critical Videos

Sha Luo1, Yogesh Prabhu2, Timothy Ossowski1, Kaiping Chen1, Junjie Hu1
1University of Wisconsin, Madison 2University of California, San Diego
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

The Problem. Current video-language models are often tested on clips where the accident is already obvious, which does not reflect real-world safety needs.

The Solution. We introduce RiskCueBench, a benchmark centered on the Risk Signalâ€”the earliest point of concern in an evolving scene.

The Data. Expert-annotated videos spanning driving incidents and protests, with labels grounded in observable cues.

The Result. A substantial performance gap remains: current systems still struggle to anticipate impending risk from temporally unfolding visual evidence.

Related Links

There's a lot of excellent work that was introduced around the same time as ours.

Progressive Encoding for Neural Optimization introduces an idea similar to our windowed position encoding for coarse-to-fine optimization.

D-NeRF and NR-NeRF both use deformation fields to model non-rigid scenes.

Some works model videos with a NeRF by directly modulating the density, such as Video-NeRF, NSFF, and DyNeRF

There are probably many more by the time you are reading this. Check out Frank Dellart's survey on recent NeRF papers, and Yen-Chen Lin's curated list of NeRF papers.
