RiskCueBench

A Benchmark for Risk Cue Recognition in Safety-Critical Videos

Sha Luo1, Yogesh Prabhu2, Timothy Ossowski1, Kaiping Chen1, Junjie Hu1
1University of Wisconsin, Madison 2University of California, San Diego
Paper arXiv Code Data

The Problem. Current video-language models are often tested on clips where the accident is already obvious, which does not reflect real-world safety needs.

The Solution. We introduce RiskCueBench, a benchmark centered on the Risk Signal, the earliest point of concern in an evolving scene.

The Data. Expert-annotated videos spanning driving incidents and protests, with labels grounded in observable cues.

The Result. A substantial performance gap remains: current systems still struggle to anticipate impending risk from temporally unfolding visual evidence.

Highlights

  • Early Risk Signal Benchmark. RiskCueBench evaluates anticipatory reasoning from risk signal clips—the earliest moments that indicate a potential safety concern—rather than post-hoc access to the full incident.
  • Real-World, Multi-Domain Data. Expert-annotated videos spanning traffic incidents and protests, with timestamps for signal boundaries and structured descriptions of visual indicators.
  • Scalable Construction Pipeline. A reproducible workflow combining structured event queries, LLM-based relevance and visual-quality filtering, and disagreement-based hard-example mining to surface ambiguous cases.
  • QRA Evaluation + Reasoning Diagnostics. Models respond in a Question–Reasoning–Answer format and are evaluated beyond F1 using metrics for reasoning grounding, temporal sensitivity, and self-correction effects.
  • Key Findings. Current VLMs lag far behind humans, perform markedly worse on protests than car crashes, show limited reliance on temporal order, and often produce poorly grounded explanations; self-correction frequently reduces accuracy.
